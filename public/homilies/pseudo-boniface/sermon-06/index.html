On the Capital Sins and the Chief Commandments of God

Sermo VI: De capitalibus peccatis et praecipuis Dei praeceptis

Pseudo-Boniface 8th–9th century AD Latin
Latin · Original
English · Translation

Original Text

Audite et intelligite, carissimi, hoc est verbum fidei quod praedicamus, ut credamus in Deum Patrem omnipotentem, et in Iesum Christum filium eius unicum Dominum nostrum, et credamus in Spiritum sanctum unum Deum omnipotentem in unitate et Trinitate, trinum in personis et nominibus, et unum in deitate maiestatis et potestatis. Hoc est verbum fidei quod praedicamus, ut discernamus inter bonum et malum, inter pium et impium, inter iustitiam et iniquitatem, id est, inter capitalia peccata et inter capitalia et principalia praecepta et mandata Dei. Haec enim sunt capitalia peccata. Sacrilegium quod dicitur cultura idolorum. Omnia autem sacrificia et auguria paganorum, sacrilegia sunt, quemadmodum sunt sacrificia mortuorum defuncta corpora, vel super sepulcra illorum, sive auguria, sive phylacteria, sive quae immolant super petras, sive ad fontes, sive ad arbores, Iovi, vel Mercurio, vel aliis diis paganorum, quae omnia daemonia sunt, et multa alia quae enumerare longum est, quae universa, iuxta iudicium sanctorum Patrum, sacrilegia a Christianis vitanda et detestanda, et capitalia peccata esse dignoscuntur. Homicidium, adulterium, fornicatio, sive cum humana natura, sive cum animalibus et pecoribus, sive masculi cum masculis, sive feminis cum feminis, ardentes in desideriis invicem. Haec omnia capitalia peccata sunt. Furta et rapinae, falsum testimonium, periurium, detractio, cupiditas, superbia, invidia, odium, vana gloria, ebrietas, ista omnia capitalia peccata esse absque dubitatione comprobantur. Haec sunt quae mergunt homines in interitum et perditionem. Ista est malitia quam sanctus Petrus abjicere iussit, quando dicit: Hoc est verbum quod evangelizatum est vobis. Deponentes igitur omnem malitiam et omne dolum. De talibus dicit Paulus apostolus: Qui talia agunt digni sunt morte; non solum qui faciunt, sed etiam qui consentiunt facientibus. Haec sunt opera Satanae, quibus abrenuntiant Christiani in baptismo. Haec opera Satanae in die exitus nostri in nobis requirit, et si invenerit, eos reos sibi vindicat, et ad inferni claustra et ad aeternas poenas locum eos nudos et flentes et lugentes trahit, ubi mors semper patitur, et nunquam per mortem maligna vita finietur, ubi ardor sulfureis flammis exaestuans et algor tremitibus ac lugentibus animabus intolerabilis et nunquam terminatur; ubi tenebrae sine luce patientur oculi eorum, qui hic lucernam Domini, id est, sanctum Evangelium, videre oculis cordis noluerunt; ubi aures nihil nisi luctum et gemitum audient, quae hic praecepta vitae in Evangelio Christi audire noluerunt; ubi esuriunt et sitiunt in aeternum, qui hic esurientibus et sitientibus miseris cibum et potum dare noluerunt; ubi semper mors optatur, sed non dabitur, ab his qui hic de morte sua cogitare noluerunt. Ibi generaliter omne malum invenitur, et nullum bonum videbitur.
Praecepta Dei sunt fides recta et vita immaculata. Fides recta fides catholica est, quam supra exposuimus. Dilectio Dei, id est, Dominum Deum diligere ex toto corde, et ex tota anima, et ex tota virtute. Deinde proximum tanquam semetipsum. Timor Domini, sicut scriptum est: Initium sapientiae timor Domini. Pax, benignitas, patientia, humilitas, continentia, modestia, iustitia, misericordia. Opera enim sunt misericordiae, esurienti cibum dare, sitienti potum, nudum vestire, infirmos et in carcere inclusos visitare, et illis ministrare, hospites suscipere, mortuos sepelire, iuste iudicare, iustitiam laudare, iniquitatem detestare et non facere, viduas et orphanos adiuvare, peregrinos suscipere, eleemosynas in pauperes partiri, tribulationem patientibus subvenire, decimas annis singulis reddere, semper orare in omni loco dominationis eius, castitatem servare et ieiunium amare, pacem semper diligere, gratiarum actionem Deo de omnibus agere. Haec facientibus et implentibus regna praeparantur aeterna. Hiis Salvator mundi in die iudicii dicturus est: Venite, benedicti Patris mei, possidete regnum quod vobis paratum est ab origine mundi; tunc fulgebunt iusti sicut sol in regno patris eorum. Ubi est lux sine tenebris, et vita sine morte, ubi est laetitia sempiterna et gaudium sine fine, ubi maiora et meliora bona spiritualia quam lingua humana exponere possit, sicut scriptum est, quod nec oculus vidit, nec auris audivit, nec in cor hominis ascendit, quae praeparavit Deus diligentibus se.

English Translation

Hear and understand, dearest brethren: this is the word of faith we preach — that we believe in God the Father almighty, and in Jesus Christ his only Son our Lord, and that we believe in the Holy Spirit, one almighty God in unity and Trinity, three in persons and names, and one in the deity of his majesty and power. This is the word of faith we preach: that we distinguish between good and evil, between the godly and the ungodly, between justice and iniquity — that is, between the capital sins on the one hand, and the capital and chief commandments and precepts of God on the other. These are the capital sins. Sacrilege, which is called the worship of idols. All sacrifices and divinations of the pagans are sacrilege — such as offerings made to the dead over their corpses, or at their graves; divinations; charms; what they sacrifice on rocks, or at springs, or at trees, to Jupiter or Mercury or the other gods of the pagans, all of which are demonic — and many other things that would take too long to list. All of these, by the judgment of the holy Fathers, are recognized as sacrileges that Christians must shun and detest, and as capital sins. Murder, adultery, fornication — whether with human nature or with beasts and animals, or men with men, or women with women, burning with desire for one another — all these are capital sins. Theft and rapine, false testimony, perjury, detraction, covetousness, pride, envy, hatred, vainglory, drunkenness: all these are established beyond doubt to be capital sins. These are what plunge men into destruction and perdition. This is the malice that holy Peter commanded to be cast away when he says:

This is the word which by the gospel hath been preached unto you. 1 Pet 1:25 Wherefore laying away all malice, and all guile, and dissimulations, and envies, and all detractions. 1 Pet 2:1 Of such people the Apostle Paul says: They who do such things are worthy of death; and not only they that do them, but they also that consent to them that do them. Rom 1:32 These are the works of Satan, which Christians renounce at baptism. On the day of our departure Satan demands an accounting of these works, and if he finds them in us he claims them as his own, dragging souls — naked, weeping, and wailing — to the gates of hell and to eternal punishment. There, death is always endured yet the wicked life never ends through death; the blazing heat seething with sulphurous flames and the cold intolerable to trembling, lamenting souls never ends; eyes that refused here to see with the eyes of the heart the lamp of the Lord, that is the holy Gospel, will suffer there in darkness without light; ears that refused here to hear the precepts of life in the Gospel of Christ will hear there nothing but weeping and groaning; those who refused here to give food and drink to the hungry and thirsty poor will hunger and thirst there forever; those who refused here to think about their own death will long there forever for death — but it will not be granted. There every evil is found, and no good will be seen.

The commandments of God are right faith and an unspotted life. Right faith is the Catholic faith, which we have set out above. Love of God — that is, loving the Lord God with one’s whole heart, whole soul, and whole strength; and then one’s neighbor as oneself. Fear of the Lord, as it is written: the beginning of wisdom is the fear of the Lord. Peace, kindness, patience, humility, continence, modesty, justice, mercy. The works of mercy are: giving food to the hungry, drink to the thirsty, clothing to the naked; visiting the sick and those imprisoned and ministering to them; receiving strangers; burying the dead; judging justly; praising justice; detesting iniquity and not committing it; helping widows and orphans; receiving pilgrims; distributing alms to the poor; relieving those in tribulation; paying tithes every year; always praying in every place where he has dominion; preserving chastity and loving fasting; always cherishing peace; giving thanks to God for all things. For those who do and fulfill these things, eternal kingdoms are prepared. To these the Savior of the world will say on the day of judgment:

Come, ye blessed of my Father, possess you the kingdom prepared for you from the foundation of the world. Matt 25:34 Then shall the just shine as the sun in the kingdom of their Father. Matt 13:43 There is light without darkness and life without death; there is everlasting joy and happiness without end; there are spiritual goods greater and better than human language can express — as it is written: Eye hath not seen, nor ear heard, neither hath it entered into the heart of man, what things God hath prepared for them that love him. 1 Cor 2:9
