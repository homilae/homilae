An Exhortation on the Lenten Fasts

Sermo XII: Exhortatio de ieiuniis Quadragesimae

Pseudo-Boniface 8th–9th century AD Latin
Latin · Original
English · Translation

Original Text

Nos sumus constituti, dilectissimi fratres, ut vobis viam salutis vestrae ostendamus, prout divina clementia intelligentiam nobis dare dignatur. Ideo rogamus ut intenta mente et pia devotione Domini mandata in omni hora diligenter facere studeatis, ut, in charitate divina firmati, nullis tentationibus ab illa separemini, sed semper facientes quod bonum est, abundetis in spe et virtute Spiritus sancti, ut per eius gratiam fidem quam accepistis et baptisma conservare valeatis ante conspectum summi Dei et Salvatoris nostri Iesu Christi, quia creati sumus ad laudem sui sancti nominis, qui dilexit nos et lavit nos a peccatis nostris in sanguine suo, quia mortem pro nobis sustinuit, ut per suam mortem destrueret eum qui habebat mortis imperium, id est, diabolum, et humanum genus ab eius potestate eripieus, aperuit credentibus regna caelorum, ubi perfruuntur vitam aeternam fideles, fructum maximum habentes aeternae vitae. Maximus fructus est solus Deus, vitae aeternae remunerator, et ideo secundum solum Deum et Dominum nostrum adoremus, et ipsi soli serviamus, ut ipse nos remunerare dignetur fructum amplissimum. Fugiamus omnia quae diabolicae subiacent potestati, ut, divina virtute roborati, praevaleamus contra eius antiquas nequitias.
Debemus, charissimi fratres, misericordiam redemptoris nostri Domini Iesu Christi omni devotione semper laudare, et pia intentione eius praecepto nos subdere, cum bona voluntate et charitate sincera, maxime tamen in istis sanctis diebus quae nunc incipiunt, animam nostram et corpus ieiunando, orando, eleemosynam dando, maiestati divinae committere, quia pro nostra salute Iesus Christus Dominus noster ad homines veniens, ieiunavit quadraginta diebus et quadraginta noctibus, et nullum omnino cibum sumpsit. Nos quoque, in quantum possumus, anno Quadragesimae tempore, carnem nostram per abstinentiam affligere conamur, quia opus est nobis ut ab illicitis cogitationibus verbisque et factis ieiunemus, viventes in caelestibus spiritualibusque praeceptis. Lege Mosaica universo populo est praeceptum decimas et primitias offerre Domino Deo. Ita debemus principia voluntatum, consummationesque operum nostrorum ad Dei gratiam offerre, et in nostro corpore persolvere decimas dierum anni, nunc in isto sacratissimo tempore, quia isti sunt dies qui constituuntur ad hoc, ut carnis nostrae decimas in his Deo demus. A praesenti enim die, ut nostis, charissimi fratres, usque in pascha dies quadraginta et duo fiunt, quorum triginta sex ieiunamus, et sex dominicis cum laetitia usi sumus victum nostrum. Dum vero per trecentos sexaginta dies annus ducitur, totum anni tempus xxxvi dierum numero decimatur. His diebus oportet nos ad ecclesiam redire, actuumque nostrorum operatione, mundo corde et casto corpore in conspectu divinae maiestatis humiliter offerre, rogando Deum omnipotentem, ut nobis indulgere dignetur, quod negligentius totum annum viximus quam debuimus. Haec, fratres charissimi, sapienter ac fideliter cogitantes, ad poenitentis remedia confugere festinemus, et per castitatem, per opera iustitiae et misericordiae, aeterna nobis apud Deum praemia comparemus. Eo diligentius debemus dies istos bene operando peragere, quia magnam indulgentiam peccatorum nostrorum apud Deum merentur ieiunia, si propter amorem Dei aguntur, et non per gloriam inanem.
Hoc est perfectum ieiunium et rationabile, quando corpus nostrum ieiunat, anima orat, facilius per ieiunium oratio penetrat caelum. Tunc enim homo spiritualis effectus angelis coniungitur. Per hoc enim prosternuntur vitia, humiliabitur caro, diaboli tentamenta vincuntur. Nolite negligere animam vestram, sed sicut carni vestrae, ne deliciat, cibos quotidie praebetis, sic mentis vestrae quotidiana alimenta bona sint opera. Cibo corpus pascitur, pio opere spiritus nutritur; quod moriturae carni tribuitis, victurae in perpetuum animae non negetis. Vita corporis anima est: vita animae Deus est, et sicut corpus mortuum est sine anima, ita anima mortua est sine Deo. Ideo Iesum Christum salvatorem nostrum habeamus in corde per opera bona, ut animam nostram et corpus conservare dignetur. Rectum est unicuique diligere Dominum Deum ex toto corde, ex tota mente, totaque virtute, et proximum sicut seipsum; nullusque hoc alteri faciat, quod sibi fieri non vult. Idcirco, charissimi, videte, ut mundetis corda vestra, et ut castigetis corpora vestra, ut digni habeamini Spiritum sanctum accipere, cuius habitatio et domus estis, recte credentes et bene agentes, non mortalis, sed aeterna spiritualis. Vigilate, fratres, in omni opere bono, retinete in memoria quae audistis in praedicatione salutis vestrae, et facite quae praecepita sunt. Habete Christum in corde, et signum sanctae crucis in fronte. Multos habemus inimicos invisibiles, qui cursum nostrum impedire contendunt, et laqueos subversionis suae ponunt in itineribus nostris, ut irretitos dilectionibus noxiis gressum vitae nobis intercludant. Contra hos signaculo crucis Christi armate vos, quia hoc est quod fugiunt et timent maligni spiritus, inimici nostri, quia per hoc signum illi damnati sunt, per hoc et nos liberati sumus. Hoc ad omne opus primum ponamus, hoc dormientes muniat et vigilantes armet, ne nobis insidians hostis dormientibus vel vigilantibus in aliquo negotio possit nocere. Nam et rex noster, hoc est Iesus Christus, si nos viderit pro quacunque tribulatione vel metu ad suum convolare vexillum, statim nos dextra potentiae suae ab omni adversitate in laudem et gloriam nominis sui sancti eruit et proteget. Est namque laudandus et colendus verus Deus, qui sperantes in se custodit. Illum rogemus, dilectissimi fratres, ut nos hic protegat et ad vitam perducat aeternam. Ecce nunc tempus acceptabile, ecce nunc dies salutis.

English Translation

We have been appointed, dearest brethren, to show you the way of your salvation, to the extent that divine mercy deigns to grant us understanding. We ask you therefore to strive at every hour, with attentive minds and devout hearts, to carry out the commandments of the Lord diligently, so that, grounded in divine charity, you may not be separated from it by any temptations, but always doing what is good, may abound in hope and in the power of the Holy Spirit. Through his grace you will be able to preserve before the sight of the most high God and our Savior Jesus Christ the faith and baptism you have received — for we were created for the praise of his holy name,

who loved us, and washed us from our sins in his own blood Rev 1:5 , because he endured death for us, so that through death he might destroy him who had the empire of death, that is to say, the devil Heb 2:14 , and rescuing the human race from his power, he opened to believers the kingdom of heaven, where the faithful fully enjoy eternal life, having the greatest fruit of eternal life. God alone is the greatest fruit, the rewarder of eternal life; therefore let us worship him alone, the Lord our God, and serve him alone, that he may deign to reward us with the most abundant fruit. Let us flee everything subject to the power of the devil, so that, strengthened by divine power, we may prevail against his ancient wickedness.
We must, dearest brethren, always praise the mercy of our redeemer the Lord Jesus Christ with all devotion, and submit ourselves to his commandment with devout intent, with good will and sincere charity — most especially in these holy days now beginning, committing our souls and bodies to the divine majesty through fasting, prayer, and almsgiving. For Jesus Christ our Lord, coming to mankind for our salvation, fasted forty days and forty nights and took no food at all. We too, as much as we can, endeavor in the season of Lent each year to afflict our flesh through abstinence, for we need to fast from illicit thoughts, words, and deeds, living by heavenly and spiritual commandments. By the Mosaic law the whole people is commanded to offer tithes and firstfruits to the Lord God. So we ought to offer the beginnings of our intentions and the completion of our works to the grace of God, and to pay in our bodies the tithes of the days of the year now in this most sacred season — for these are the days set aside for us to give God the tithe of our flesh. From this present day, as you know, dearest brethren, to Easter there are forty-two days, of which we fast for thirty-six, and on the six Sundays we have joyfully taken our food. Since the year runs for three hundred and sixty days, the whole year is tithed by the number of thirty-six days. During these days we must return to the church and humbly offer to the divine majesty, through the work of our deeds, with a clean heart and a chaste body, asking Almighty God to deign to pardon us for having lived all year more negligently than we should have. Thinking wisely and faithfully on these things, dearest brethren, let us hasten to take refuge in the remedies of penance, and through chastity, through works of justice and mercy, let us lay up eternal rewards for ourselves with God. We ought to carry out these days by acting well all the more diligently, because fasts earn great pardon of our sins before God, provided they are undertaken for love of God and not for empty glory.

This is the perfect and reasonable fast: when the body fasts and the soul prays, for prayer more easily pierces heaven through fasting. Then the person, made spiritual, is joined to the angels. Through this, vices are laid low; the flesh is humbled; the temptations of the devil are overcome. Do not neglect your soul. Just as you provide food daily to your flesh so that it does not become weak, so let good works be the daily nourishment of your mind. The body is fed by food; the spirit is nourished by devout action. What you grant to the flesh that is going to die, do not refuse to the soul that is going to live forever. The life of the body is the soul; the life of the soul is God — and just as the body is dead without the soul, so the soul is dead without God. Let us therefore hold Jesus Christ our savior in our heart through good works, that he may deign to preserve our soul and body. It is right for each person to love the Lord God with the whole heart, the whole mind, and the whole strength, and one’s neighbor as oneself; and let no one do to another what he does not want done to himself. Therefore, dearest brethren, see to it that you cleanse your hearts and discipline your bodies, so that you may be found worthy to receive the Holy Spirit, whose dwelling and house you are — believing rightly and acting well, not in a mortal and passing way, but in an eternal and spiritual one.

Be vigilant, brethren, in every good work; keep in memory what you have heard in the preaching of your salvation, and do what has been commanded. Hold Christ in your heart and the sign of the holy cross on your forehead. We have many invisible enemies who strive to impede our course and place snares of their own making in our paths, to entangle us in harmful attachments and block the step of our life. Against them arm yourselves with the seal of the cross of Christ, because this is what the evil spirits — our enemies — flee and fear: for it was through this sign that they were condemned, and through it we were freed. Let us place it first at the start of every action; let it protect us as we sleep and arm us as we keep watch, so that the enemy who lies in ambush for us may not be able to harm us, whether sleeping or waking, in any affair. Our King, Jesus Christ, if he sees us flying to his standard in any tribulation or fear, will immediately snatch us by the right hand of his power from all adversity, to the praise and glory of his holy name, and will protect us. For the true God, who guards those who hope in him, is worthy of praise and honor. Let us pray to him, dearest brethren, that he may protect us here and lead us to eternal life.

Behold, now is the acceptable time; behold, now is the day of salvation. 2 Cor 6:2
