About Homiliae

An open-access archive of historical Catholic preaching.

What is this?

Homiliae is a free, open-access archive of Catholic homilies and sermons delivered before 1900. Every text is presented in its original language alongside an English translation, displayed side by side so that readers can follow both at once.

The archive spans the full tradition of Catholic preaching — from the Fathers of the early Church, through the great medieval homiliaries, to the Counter-Reformation preachers and the bishops and priests of the modern Catholic world. The vast majority of the texts here have never previously been available in English.

How are translations made?

All translations in this archive are produced using AI language models. Every translation is reviewed by the editor before publication.

We use AI translation for two reasons. First, accuracy: modern frontier AI models handle Latin, Greek, French, Spanish, Italian, and Portuguese with a high level of precision and theological accuracy. Second, cost: professional translation of on the scale needed for this archive would be prohibitively expensive.

Every translation is labelled as AI-assisted. These translations should not be cited in academic work without independent verification against the original. For devotional reading, catechesis, and general study, they are reliable. Errors and corrections are always welcome.

Texts and copyright

All original-language texts in this archive are in the public domain.

All translations are newly produced for this archive and are are in the public domain. You are free to use, share, and adapt them for any purpose, including commercial use.

Corrections and contributions

This is a small project built by one person. Errors will exist. If you find a mistranslation, a wrong attribution, or a source problem, please write to:

homiliae.archive@gmail.com

If you are a scholar and would like to contribute a translation or review existing ones, that help is very warmly welcomed.

The project

Homiliae is an independent, non-commercial project with the aim of making the preaching tradition of the Catholic Church freely available in English. It is built with Hugo and hosted on GitHub Pages. All content is free, ad-free, and always will be.
