## Original Text

{{< section "1" >}}
Cogitemus, dilectissimi fratres, assidue qualiter vivamus in hac praesenti vita, qualiter victuri simus post huius vitae terminum. Nullatenus itaque homines perire possunt, sicut animalia ratione carentia, sed omnis homo animam habet aeternam, quae iudicabitur secundum ea quae gessit in corpore, sive bonum, sive malum. Salus est enim uniuscuiusque Dei obedire mandatis, illiusque tota intentione semper facere voluntatem, qui vult omnes homines salvos fieri et neminem perire. Ideoque post baptismi sacramentum secundam poenitentiam posuit purgationem, ut in ea quae post baptismi ablutionem agimus, poenitentiae medicamentis sanentur; ut qui prius in malis actibus fuimus, postea conversi in bonis operibus vivamus, quia non sufficit tantummodo mala derelinquere, nisi etiam mox et bona quae mandata sunt nobis faciamus, dicente propheta: Diverte a malo, et fac bonum. Nam superbiae malum humilitatis bono immutandum est, ut qui fuimus per superbiam diaboli sectatores, per humilitatem simus imitatores Christi. Igitur per superbiam angelicae potestates de caelesti ceciderunt gloria, per humilitatem vero Christi humanum genus ad caelestem vocatur gloriam. Initium omnis peccati superbia est, qua homo despexit mandatis obedire Dei sui. Inde fuit ruina primi hominis, quia contempsit mandato obedire Creatoris sui; et si ille primus homo per inobedientiam de paradisi eiectus est gaudiis, nos vero per obedientiam mandatorum Dei ad vitam valeamus pervenire perpetuam, dicente Domino in Evangelio cuidam se interroganti quomodo vitam aeternam possidere potuisset: Si vis vitam ingredi, serva mandata; et in his mandatis Domini maximum est ac iustissimum: Diliges Dominum Deum tuum ex toto corde tuo, et ex tota anima tua, et ex omnibus viribus tuis. Secundum simile est huic: Diliges proximum tuum sicut te ipsum, in quo est: Quod tibi non vis, alteri ne facias; quod ipsa natura hominis docet, quia nullus vult homo ut ei alter noceat. Ideo nulli debet ille nocere, et sicut nos desideramus ut alii nos adiuvent in nostris necessitatibus, ita et alios debemus adiuvare in suis necessitatibus secundum virium nostrarum possibilitatem, et benigni esse et misericordes ad omnes homines, sicut Deus Pater benignus est et misericors in nos, qui solem suum facit oriri super bonos et malos, et ad fructificanda semina pluviam dabit terrae nostrae, ut terra germinet et faciat fructum unde nostrae mortalitatis vita sustentetur. Ideoque necessarium est illum diligere, et eius obedire mandatis, ut per eius pietatem ab omnibus protegamur adversis, et aeternis per eum valeamus abundare bonis. Nunquam enim illius nos deserit bonitas, si illius nos non derelinquamus voluntatem, et voluntas illius salus est nobis et prosperitas ac beatitudo aeterna.
{{< /section >}}

{{< section "2" >}}
Ideo tota virtute fugiamus vitia et crimina quae diabolus suadet, facientes bona quae Deus ipse nobis praecepit facere, hoc est dilectionem Dei et proximi, sicut praediximus, pietatem et misericordiam in miseros. Non simus avari ad congregandum vel servandum, sed benigni ad largiendum, quia propriae divitiae redemptio est viri, et donorum quae in pauperes fiunt Christus erit remunerator. Sicut aqua exstinguit ignem, ita eleemosyna delet peccatum. Similiter ieiuniis, et orationibus, et abstinentia carnalium desideriorum, et in confessione et poenitentia omnia possunt deleri peccata. Sed nullus in peccatis suis securus iaceat, quia nemo ultimum praescire poterit diem, sed surgat per confessionem et poenitentiam, et revertatur ad dominum Deum suum, quia pius est ad ignoscendum, si nos pigri non erimus ad poenitendum. Casti simus et sobrii in corpore nostro, fornicationes et alias fugientes immunditias corporales et omnes delectationes iniustas depellamus a nobis. Mundemus corda et corpora nostra in conspectu Domini Dei nostri, ut digni simus habitaculum Spiritui sancto.
Furta et rapinas, falsa testimonia, et periuria, et homicidia, et his similia crimina nullus vestrum faciat, ne quisquam contra alium invidiam habeat, nec inimicitiam, nec dissensiones occultas, sed pacifice conversetur cum omnibus, Domino dicente: Beati pacifici quoniam Filii Dei vocabuntur. Comessationes, et ebrietates, et inania verba, et turpiloquium omnimodis cavete vobis: quia ebriosi, ut dicit Apostolus, regnum Dei non possidebunt. Auguria et incantationes et ligaturas nolite exercere, nec illis credere, quia diabolica haec sunt argumenta, non Domini praecepta; sed ad Christi ecclesiam concurrite, ibi sanitatem corporum et salutem animarum quaerite vestrarum. In Dei enim omnipotentis potestate salus et vita omnium nostrorum est, cuius pietas nunquam deserit in se sperantes, quapropter per illius bonitatem speremus et credamus omnia nobis bona venire. Patientes simus, et nulli malum pro malo reddentes, sed indulgeamus in nos peccantibus, ut divina misericordia nobis dignetur indulgere delicta nostra, sicut in Dominica Oratione solemus dicere. Semper Deum habeamus ubique praesentem, et si coram homine timemus peccare, quanto magis coram oculis Dei, qui omnia evidentissimo conspicit, quae nos cogitamus vel loquimur, vel agimus. Illum ubique habemus testem in vita nostra, illum post hanc vitam iudicem habemus, illius semper tota dilectione et tota virtute impleamus voluntatem, quatenus ille nobis gloriam aeternam cum sanctis suis concedere dignetur, ut dum impii pro peccatis et sceleribus suis flammis cum diabolo tradentur aeternis, nos pro bonis operibus cum Christo et coelitibus angelicis gaudia aeternae beatitudinis, divina donante gratia, intrare mereamur.
{{< /section >}}

## English Translation

{{< section "1" >}}
Let us consider unceasingly, most beloved brethren, how we live in this present life, and how we must live after this life ends. People cannot perish in the way of brute animals that lack reason, for every person has an immortal soul that will be judged according to what it has done in the body, whether good or evil. The salvation of each person consists in obeying the commandments of God and always doing his will with complete intent — he who wills all people to be saved and none to perish. And so after the sacrament of baptism he has appointed a second remedy of purgation through penance, so that what we do after the washing of baptism may be healed by the medicines of repentance — so that we who were formerly engaged in evil deeds may, being converted, live henceforth in good works. It is not enough simply to abandon evil unless we also straightway do the good things commanded of us, as the prophet says: {{< verse ref="Ps 36:27" >}}Turn away from evil, and do good.{{< /verse >}} The evil of pride must be exchanged for the good of humility, so that we who were followers of the devil through pride may become imitators of Christ through humility. Through pride the angelic powers fell from their heavenly glory; through the humility of Christ the human race is called to heavenly glory. Pride is the beginning of all sin — it is what caused man to despise obeying the commandments of his God. It was the ruin of the first man: he scorned to obey the commandment of his Creator. If that first man was cast out of the joys of Paradise through disobedience, then through obedience to God's commandments may we attain to everlasting life — the Lord saying in the Gospel to someone who asked how he could possess eternal life: {{< verse ref="Matt 19:17" >}}If thou wilt enter into life, keep the commandments.{{< /verse >}} The greatest and most just among these commandments of the Lord is: {{< verse ref="Matt 22:37-39" >}}Thou shalt love the Lord thy God with thy whole heart, and with thy whole soul, and with all thy strength. And the second is like to this: Thou shalt love thy neighbour as thyself{{< /verse >}} — which includes: do not do to another what you do not want done to yourself. Human nature itself teaches this, because no one wants another to harm them. Therefore no one should harm anyone, and just as we want others to help us in our needs, so we ought to help others in their needs as best we can, being kind and merciful to all people — as God the Father is kind and merciful toward us, {{< verse ref="Matt 5:45" >}}who maketh his sun to rise upon the good, and bad{{< /verse >}}, and gives rain to our earth for the germination of seed, so that the earth may spring up and bring forth fruit by which our mortal life is sustained. It is therefore necessary to love him and obey his commandments, so that through his mercy we may be protected from all adversity and through him may abound in eternal goods. His goodness never abandons us if we do not abandon his will, and his will is our salvation, our prosperity, and our eternal blessedness.
{{< /section >}}

{{< section "2" >}}
With all our strength, therefore, let us flee the vices and crimes the devil urges, and do the good things God himself has commanded us — that is, love of God and neighbor, as we have said, piety and mercy toward the wretched. Let us not be greedy to accumulate and hoard, but generous to give, because {{< verse ref="Prov 13:8" >}}the ransom of a man's life are his riches{{< /verse >}}, and Christ will be the rewarder of gifts made to the poor. {{< verse ref="Sir 3:33" >}}As water quencheth fire, so alms extinguish sin.{{< /verse >}} Likewise through fasting, prayer, abstinence from carnal desires, and through confession and penance, all sins can be blotted out. But let no one lie complacent in their sins, because no one will be able to foresee their last day; let them instead rise through confession and penance and return to the Lord their God, who is merciful to forgive if we are not too sluggish to repent. Let us be chaste and sober in our bodies; let us flee fornication and other bodily impurities and drive from ourselves all unjust pleasures. Let us cleanse our hearts and bodies in the sight of the Lord our God, so that we may be worthy to be a dwelling-place of the Holy Spirit.

Let none of you commit theft and rapine, false testimonies, perjuries, murders, and similar crimes. Let no one harbor envy against another, or enmity, or secret dissensions, but live peaceably with everyone, the Lord saying: {{< verse ref="Matt 5:9" >}}Blessed are the peacemakers: for they shall be called the children of God.{{< /verse >}} Guard yourselves in every way against gluttony, drunkenness, idle words, and foul speech, for drunkards, as the Apostle says, {{< verse ref="1 Cor 6:10" >}}shall not possess the kingdom of God.{{< /verse >}} Do not practice divinations, incantations, and charms, or believe in them, because these are the contrivances of the devil, not the commandments of the Lord. Rather hasten to the church of Christ; there seek the health of your bodies and the salvation of your souls. For in the power of Almighty God lies the salvation and life of us all — whose mercy never abandons those who hope in him — and therefore through his goodness let us hope and believe that all good things come to us. Let us be patient, repaying no one evil for evil, but forgiving those who sin against us, so that divine mercy may deign to forgive us our offenses, as we are accustomed to say in the Lord's Prayer. Let us always hold God as present everywhere; and if we fear to sin before a fellow human being, how much more should we fear before the eyes of God, who sees with perfect clarity everything we think, say, or do. We have him everywhere as a witness in our lives; we will have him as judge after this life. Let us always fulfill his will with our whole love and our whole strength, so that he may deign to grant us eternal glory with his saints — that while the wicked are handed over to eternal flames with the devil for their sins and crimes, we, by the grace of God, may merit for our good works to enter the joys of eternal blessedness with Christ and the heavenly angels.
{{< /section >}}
