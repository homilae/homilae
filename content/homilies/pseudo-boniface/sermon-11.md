## Original Text

{{< section "1" >}}
Duo regna statuit Deus, praesentis dico temporis et futuri, et tempora utroque constituit, statuitque exspectare iudicii sui diem, quam ipse definivit, in qua habenda sit rerum omnium animarumque discretio, ut impii quidem pro peccatis suis igni tradantur aeterno, hi vero qui secundum voluntatem conditoris Dei vixerunt, pro bonis operibus benedictione suscepta, clarissima luce fulgentes, introducti ad aeternum saeculum, ineffabilium bonorum munera aeterna percipiant. Modo fratres dies purificationis sancta, et spirituales iam amanda sunt, in quibus aliqua corporis fatigatio, animae vero lucra inveniuntur, et ideo, sicut Apostolus dicit: Ecce nunc tempus acceptabile, ecce nunc dies salutis, ideo vacare Deo studeamus sollicite, et chara saeculi transeuntia relinquamus, et Deo pio patri adhaereamus, admonente psalmista ac dicente: Vacate et videte, quoniam ego sum Dominus. Vacare ergo Domino est laudibus eius insistere, et onera peccatorum per poenitentiam abiicere, et ad Deum conditorem convertere. Nolite imitari malos Christianos. Sunt enim, quod peius est, qui post baptisma multa crimina et peccata committunt, et medicamentum poenitentiae non requirunt, sed per omnes scelerum campos, laxatis peccatorum habenis, per praecipitia luxuriae feruntur infernis, et sine ulla compunctione vel correctione vitae ad altare accedere et communicare praesumunt. Vos vero qui baptizati estis, nolite imitari tales, ne forte si eos imitari volueritis, cum ipsis aeterna supplicia pereatis.
{{< /section >}}

{{< section "2" >}}
Custodite in vobis baptismi sacramentum et nitelatem velut inferni foveam fugite, superbiam, invidiam, vanitatem quasi gladium diaboli pertimescite. Nolite detrahere, quia, sicut scriptum est: Qui detrahit fratri suo eradicabitur de terra viventium. Nolite maledicere, quia scriptum est: Neque maledicentes regnum Dei possidebunt. Nolite falsum testimonium dicere, qui scriptum est: Testis falsus non erit impunitus. Nolite mentiri propter quod scriptum est: Os quod mentitur occidit animam. Nolite vos invicem odio habere, quia scriptum est: Qui odit fratrem suum homicida est, quia quicunque fecerit hoc mali, perdit baptismi sacramentum. Nolite facere furtum in quolibet ingenio. Fraudes nolite committere. Stateras dolosas et mensuras duplices quasi mortifera venena vitate. Ad ecclesiam frequenter convenite, sacerdotibus vestris et honorem et amorem impendite. De ipsa substantia vestra et decimas reddite, et secundum vires vestras eleemosynas facite, nolite munera super innocentes accipere, sed quotienscunque causas auditis, Deum attendite, et iustum iudicium iudicate, ne forte dum pecuniam iniuste vultis adipiscere, aeternum supplicium non possitis evadere. Qui de salute animae suae attentius cogitat, unde laeditur quantum potest fugiat; qui enim non cogitat nisi de ista tantum vita, animalibus et pecoribus similis est. Quid enim quaerunt animalia nisi manducare et bibere atque dormire? Talis est qui plus cogitat de carne sua quam de anima, qui plus diligit gulam aut luxuriam quam castitatem atque iustitiam.
{{< /section >}}

{{< section "3" >}}
Scire debetis, charissimi, quia ideo Christiani facti sumus, ut semper de futuro saeculo et de aeterna beatitudine cogitemus, et plus pro anima quam pro corpore laboremus, quia caro nostra paucis annis erit in mundo. Anima enim nostra, si bene agimus, sine fine regnabit in caelo. Poenitentiam itaque agamus, fratres, ut peccatorum propitiiatorem Deum habere mereamur. Deprecemur eum, quia exacerbavimus eum; humiliemur, ut exaltet nos; defleamus, ut laetificet nos, plangamus, ut consoletur nos; proiciamus a nobis consuetudinem malam et quasi vestimento virtute animi induamur, maxime nos qui iam conversationem angelicam promeruimus, ut ad angelorum consortium mereamur pervenire, ubi, ab auditu malo liberati, illam felicem et desiderabilem vocem mereamur audire: Enge, serve bone et fidelis, intra in gaudium Domini tui, praestante Domino nostro Iesu Christo qui vivit et regnat in saecula saeculorum. Amen.
{{< /section >}}

## English Translation

{{< section "1" >}}
God has established two kingdoms — I mean the present age and the age to come — and has set the time for each, appointing us to await the day of his judgment, which he himself has determined, on which the reckoning of all things and all souls will take place. The wicked will be handed over to eternal fire for their sins, while those who have lived according to the will of God their maker will receive blessing for their good works, shining in the brightest light, ushered into the eternal age to receive the eternal gifts of inexpressible goods. Now, brethren, the holy days of purification and the spiritual season are upon us and must be embraced — days in which some wearying of the body is found, but great profit for the soul. Therefore, as the Apostle says: {{< verse ref="2 Cor 6:2" >}}Behold, now is the acceptable time; behold, now is the day of salvation{{< /verse >}} — let us earnestly devote ourselves to God, put aside the dear but passing things of this world, and cling to God our loving Father, as the Psalmist admonishes: {{< verse ref="Ps 45:11" >}}Be still and see that I am God.{{< /verse >}} To be still for the Lord means to persist in praising him, to cast off the burdens of sin through penance, and to turn to God our maker. Do not imitate bad Christians. For there are those — and this is worse — who after baptism commit many crimes and sins and do not seek the medicine of penance, but are carried headlong into hell through all the fields of wickedness, with the reins of sin slackened, over the cliffs of lust, and yet presume to approach the altar and receive communion without any compunction or correction of life. You who are baptized — do not imitate such people, lest in wanting to imitate them you perish with them in eternal punishment.
{{< /section >}}

{{< section "2" >}}
Guard the sacrament of baptism within you and its purity of conscience — flee it as if it were the pit of hell; dread pride, envy, and vanity as if they were the sword of the devil. Do not speak ill of others, because as it is written: {{< verse ref="Ps 100:5" >}}The one who slanders his brother will be rooted out from the land of the living.{{< /verse >}} Do not curse, because it is written: {{< verse ref="1 Cor 6:10" >}}Nor cursers shall possess the kingdom of God.{{< /verse >}} Do not bear false witness, because it is written: {{< verse ref="Prov 19:5" >}}A false witness shall not be unpunished.{{< /verse >}} Do not lie, because it is written: {{< verse ref="Wis 1:11" >}}The mouth that lieth, killeth the soul.{{< /verse >}} Do not hate one another, because it is written: {{< verse ref="1 John 3:15" >}}Whosoever hateth his brother is a murderer{{< /verse >}} — for whoever commits this evil loses the sacrament of baptism. Do not steal by any means. Do not commit fraud. Avoid dishonest scales and double measures as if they were deadly poison. Come frequently to the church; show your priests both honor and love. Pay tithes from your own substance, and give alms according to your means. Do not accept bribes against the innocent, but whenever you hear cases, keep God in mind and judge a just judgment — lest in wanting to gain money unjustly, you cannot escape eternal punishment. The person who thinks carefully about the salvation of his soul should flee as far as possible from whatever harms it; the person who thinks only about this present life is like animals and brute beasts. For what do animals seek except to eat, drink, and sleep? That is what a person is like who thinks more about his flesh than his soul, who loves gluttony or lust more than chastity and justice.
{{< /section >}}

{{< section "3" >}}
You should know, dearest brethren, that we became Christians precisely so that we might always think about the age to come and about eternal blessedness, and labor more for the soul than for the body, because our flesh will be in this world for only a few years. Our soul, if we act well, will reign without end in heaven. Let us therefore do penance, brethren, so that we may merit to have God as the one who pardons our sins. Let us pray to him, because we have provoked him; let us humble ourselves, that he may exalt us; let us weep, that he may make us rejoice; let us lament, that he may console us. Let us cast off evil habits and clothe ourselves in the virtue of the soul as if in a garment — especially we who have already merited an angelic way of life, that we may merit to reach the company of the angels. There, freed from the hearing of evil, we may merit to hear that happy and longed-for voice: {{< verse ref="Matt 25:21" >}}Well done, good and faithful servant, enter thou into the joy of thy lord{{< /verse >}} — through the grace of our Lord Jesus Christ, who lives and reigns for ever and ever. Amen.
{{< /section >}}
