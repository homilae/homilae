## Original Text

{{< section "1" >}}
Prima ergo justitia est mala non facere quae diabolus suggerit. Secunda est bona facere quae Deus omnipotens hortatur nos facere, quia Deus desiderat salutem animarum nostrarum, sicut ait: Qui vult omnes homines salvos fieri. Audiamus itaque, dilectissimi, quae sint mala quae diabolus suggerit, et postea quae sint bona quae Dominus Deus noster vult ut faciamus, ut simus filii dilectionis suae, non filii perditionis aeternae. Prima et pessima in peccatis superbia, per quam diabolus, cum esset angelus clarissimus in caelo, cecidit, et factus est aeternae perditionis reus cum omnibus satellitibus suis. Secundum fuit inobedientia, per quod homo primus cecidit de paradiso, dum Dei omnipotentis mandatum non custodivit, et missus est in hanc mortalitatem, et reus factus est in omni posteritate filiorum suorum aeternae damnationis: quam damnationem, ut diximus, Deus omnipotens venit in mundum, ut averteret a nobis, et liberaret nos ab omni miseria. Deinde prohibet nos Dominus Deus diabolo obedire, quia ille desiderat multos habere socios suae perditionis, et illi qui faciunt voluntatem diaboli in fornicationibus, et homicidiis, et adulteriis, et ebrietatibus, et in falsis testimoniis, et perjurationibus, et injustitiis, et rapinis, et iniquitatibus; et qui invidiam habent et odium, et detractiones contra fratres suos, aeterna calamitate damnantur cum diabolo et angelis ejus in igne perpetuo. Qui vero omnipotentis Dei facere voluntatem et praeceptis ejus obedire, in aeterna gloria erunt regnaturi cum Christo et sanctis angelis ejus.
{{< /section >}}

{{< section "2" >}}
Haec sunt praecepta divina per quae nobis aeterna beatitudo donabitur a Deo omnipotente. Primum charitas Dei, et charitas hominum, ut diligamus Deum ex toto corde, et tota anima, et tota mente, id est, omni intelligentia nostra, et omni voluntate nostra, et semper in memoriam habeamus voluntatem et praecepta illius; et diligamus homines alios sicut nosmet ipsos, nulli facientes aliquid quod nolumus ut alter nobis faciat; sed sic faciamus in alios, sicut nostra voluntas sit, ut alii nobis faciant. Et simus misericordes in pauperes et in miseros, et faciamus illis bene secundum vires nostras et necessitatem illorum, et si quis peccat in nobis, dimittamus illi, ut nobis Deus dimittat peccata nostra, quia sic in Evangelio legimus, ipsa Veritate dicente: Si dimiseritis hominibus peccata illorum quae in vos gesserunt, dimittet et vobis Pater vester caelestis peccata vestra. Et habeamus humilitatem in conspectu Domini, quia sicut scriptum est: Humiliamini sub manu omnipotentis Dei, ut vos exaltet in tempore tribulationis, quia, sicut diximus prius, angeli de caelo per superbiam ceciderunt. Sed Dei clementia perdonavit nobis per humilitatem ascendere ad angelicas sedes. Et habeamus patientiam, sicut ipse Dominus dixit: In patientia vestra possidebitis animas vestras; et nulli malum pro malo reddentes, et castitatem corporis nostri diligenter observemus, quia Spiritus sanctus, sicut scriptum est, non habitat in corpore peccatis subdito. Nec furta faciamus, sed de nostro justo labore demus pauperibus eleemosynas, ut ipsi orent pro nobis, et Deus indulgeat nobis peccata nostra, quia propriae divitiae viri, ut legitur in Sapientia, redemptio est animae illius. Et simus obedientes doctoribus ecclesiasticis in corde puro et conscientia bona. Honoremus ecclesias Christi, et saepius veniamus ad orationes. Et honoremus parentes nostros, sicut in lege Dominus demandavit. Honora patrem tuum et matrem tuam. Sed et parentes filios suos erudiant in omni castitate et timore Dei, non consentientes illis nec furari, nec fornicari, nec inebriari, nec aliquid injuste agere. Uxores vero suos maritos timeant et honorent, et castitatem illis servent. Similiter et viri uxores suas diligant, et castitatem quam illi quaerunt ab uxoribus suis, faciant illi prius, ut videant exempla illorum bona. Pauperes vero et miseri gaudeant in sua paupertate, sperent in misericordia Dei, qui ait: Beati pauperes spiritu, quoniam ipsorum est regnum caelorum. Vivant in castitate et in fide recta, et in spe aeternae retributionis, et patienter ferant miserias, ut Deus omnipotens concedat illis aeternam beatitudinem.
{{< /section >}}

{{< section "3" >}}
Presbyteri vero et totus clerus sanctae ecclesiae, qui in servitio Dei stare debeat, die noctuque, omnimodis irreprehensibiliter vivant coram saecularibus, et in omni loco, ut bonis illorum exemplis erudiamur multi. Et sit illorum meditatio in lege Dei, et in servitio ecclesiastico die noctuque. Non sint ebriosi, nec avari, nec verbosi in conviviis, nec divitias in hoc mundo quaerentes, sed in caelesti regno aeternum sibi thesaurum praeparantes. Ostendant itaque omnibus hominibus bonae conversationis exempla, et doceant populo Christiani veritate et fide, ut cum multiplici sanctarum animarum lucro appareant in conspectu Domini Dei nostri, et multiplici remuneratione digni efficiantur.
{{< /section >}}

{{< section "4" >}}
Adolescentes itaque et juniores sint obedientes senioribus in omni doctrina spirituali, nihil sine consilio seniorum agentes. Sint subditi in humilitate et obedientia, ut digni efficiantur secundum aetatis tempus opportunum ministrare Christo Deo. Qui in adolescentia non didicerit quae bona sunt, quomodo habere vel docere poterit in senectute? Omnes enim ubique, sive divites, sive pauperes, sive seniores, sive juniores, sive domini, sive servi, sive praelati, sive subditi, Deum diligant, et illius praecepta custodiant diligenter. Sint veraces in sermonibus, justi in judiciis, misericordes in pietate, solliciti in servitio, humiles in moribus, patientes contra omnes adversitates, et fidem rectam et catholicam diligenter discant, viventes in fide et spe et charitate. Jejunia publica in castitate custodiant, et eleemosynis ornent jejunia sua, ut quod sibi detrahunt pauperibus largiantur. Decimas quoque suas fideliter reddant Domino Deo omnipotenti, ut Dominus Deus benedictionem mittat super labores suos. Ad ecclesias saepius veniant, et maxime diebus festivis, nec vaniloquium habeant in ecclesiis, sed orationibus et compunctione cordis, solliciti sint sicut decet in domo Dei et in conspectu altissimi regis. Et si quis in quolibet peccato lapsus ceciderit, citius per confessionem resurgat, et purget se in poenitentiam, quia Dei misericordia dives est ad ignoscendum peccanti, si se convertere vult ad viam justitiae, sicut ait propheta: Nolo mortem peccatoris, sed ut convertatur et vivat. Item in alio loco de voluntate et operatione novissima: In quacunque die conversus fuerit homo, vita vivet, et non morietur. Diaboli itaque invidia nos suadet peccare; sed Domini misericordia, qui salutem desiderat nostram, hortatur nos a peccato converti ad viam justitiae, ut digni esse efficiamur aeternae gloriae cum sanctis suis. Et hoc certissime scire debemus et credere, quod omnia opera nostra vel cogitationes in die novissimo judicabuntur. Peccatoribus autem qui nolunt peccata dimittere, et mandatis Dei obedire, aeterna erunt tormenta, in igne perpetuo cum diabolo et angelis suis. Qui vero se toto corde convertunt ad Deum omnipotentem, viventes in charitate, et Dei praeceptis obedientes, aeterna erit gloria, beatitudo et laetitia, cum Christo et sanctis ejus, in saecula sempiterna.
{{< /section >}}

## English Translation

{{< section "1" >}}
The first justice, then, is to avoid the evils which the devil suggests. The second is to do the good things which Almighty God urges us to do, for God desires the salvation of our souls, as He declares: {{< verse ref="1 Tim 2:4" >}}Who will have all men to be saved, and to come to the knowledge of the truth.{{< /verse >}} Hear therefore, most beloved, what are the evils that the devil suggests, and thereafter what are the good things which our Lord God wills us to do, that we may be children of His love and not children of eternal perdition. The first and worst of sins is pride, through which the devil, when he was a most brilliant angel in heaven, fell, and became guilty of eternal perdition together with all his followers. The second was disobedience, through which the first man fell from Paradise when he did not keep the commandment of Almighty God, and was sent into this mortality, and rendered guilty of eternal damnation in all the posterity of his children — which damnation, as we have said, Almighty God came into the world to turn away from us and to deliver us from all misery. Furthermore, the Lord God forbids us to obey the devil, because the devil desires to have many companions in his perdition; and those who do the will of the devil in fornications, and murders, and adulteries, and drunkenness, and in false testimonies, and perjuries, and injustices, and plunderings, and iniquities — and those who harbour envy and hatred and slanders against their brethren — are condemned to eternal calamity with the devil and his angels in everlasting fire. But those who will do the will of Almighty God and obey His commandments shall reign in eternal glory with Christ and His holy angels.
{{< /section >}}

{{< section "2" >}}
These are the divine commandments through which eternal blessedness will be granted to us by Almighty God. First is charity toward God and charity toward men: that we love God with our whole heart, and our whole soul, and our whole mind — that is, with all our understanding and all our will — and that we keep ever in memory His will and His commandments; and that we love other men as ourselves, doing to no one anything that we should not wish another to do to us, but dealing with others as our own will would have others deal with us. Let us be merciful to the poor and the wretched, and do good to them according to our means and their necessity; and if anyone sin against us, let us forgive him, that God may forgive us our sins. For so we read in the Gospel, Truth itself declaring: {{< verse ref="Matt 6:14" >}}For if you will forgive men their offences, your heavenly Father will forgive you also your offences.{{< /verse >}} And let us have humility in the sight of the Lord, for as it is written: {{< verse ref="1 Pet 5:6" >}}Be you humbled therefore under the mighty hand of God, that he may exalt you in the time of visitation.{{< /verse >}} For, as we said before, the angels fell from heaven through pride; but the clemency of God has granted us to ascend through humility to the seats of the angels. And let us have patience, as the Lord Himself said: {{< verse ref="Luke 21:19" >}}In your patience you shall possess your souls.{{< /verse >}} Let us render evil to no one for evil, and let us diligently preserve the chastity of our body, for the Holy Ghost, as it is written, dwelleth not in a body subject to sin. Let us not commit theft, but from our just labour give alms to the poor, that they may pray for us and God may pardon us our sins, for a man's own riches, as we read in Wisdom: {{< verse ref="Prov 13:8" >}}The ransom of a man's life are his riches.{{< /verse >}} Let us be obedient to the teachers of the Church with a pure heart and a good conscience. Let us honour the churches of Christ and come frequently to the prayers. And let us honour our parents, as the Lord ordained in the law: {{< verse ref="Exod 20:12" >}}Honour thy father and thy mother, that thou mayst be long lived upon the land which the Lord thy God will give thee.{{< /verse >}} Moreover, let parents instruct their children in all chastity and the fear of God, not consenting that they steal, or fornicate, or become drunk, or do anything unjust. Let wives fear and honour their husbands and preserve chastity toward them. Likewise let husbands love their wives, and let them first practise themselves the chastity which they seek from their wives, that their own good example may be seen. And let the poor and wretched rejoice in their poverty and hope in the mercy of God, who says: {{< verse ref="Matt 5:3" >}}Blessed are the poor in spirit: for theirs is the kingdom of heaven.{{< /verse >}} Let them live in chastity and in right faith, and in hope of eternal recompense, and patiently bear their hardships, that Almighty God may grant them eternal blessedness.
{{< /section >}}

{{< section "3" >}}
As for priests and the whole clergy of the holy Church, who ought to stand in the service of God day and night, let them live blamelessly in every respect before the laity, and in every place, so that many may be instructed by their good example. Let their meditation be in the law of God and in the divine office day and night. Let them not be given to drunkenness, nor to avarice, nor to idle talk at table, nor seeking riches in this world, but preparing for themselves an everlasting treasure in the heavenly kingdom. Let them show to all men the example of a good way of life, and teach the Christian people in truth and faith, so that they may appear before the face of our Lord God with the manifold gain of holy souls, and be found worthy of manifold reward.
{{< /section >}}

{{< section "4" >}}
Let young men and juniors be obedient to their elders in all spiritual teaching, doing nothing without the counsel of their elders. Let them be submissive in humility and obedience, that they may be found worthy, when the fitting time of life arrives, to minister to Christ God. For he who in his youth has not learned what is good, how shall he possess it or teach it in old age? Let all everywhere — whether rich or poor, elder or young, master or servant, prelate or subject — love God and diligently keep His commandments. Let them be truthful in their words, just in their judgements, merciful in piety, diligent in service, humble in conduct, patient under all adversities; and let them diligently learn the right and catholic faith, living in faith and hope and charity. Let them observe the public fasts in chastity and adorn their fasting with almsgiving, so that what they take from themselves they may give to the poor. Let them also faithfully render their tithes to the Lord God Almighty, that the Lord God may send blessing upon their labours. Let them come frequently to the churches, and most of all on feast days, and let them not engage in vain talk in the churches, but be attentive in prayers and compunction of heart, as befits the house of God and the presence of the most high King. And if anyone has fallen into any sin, let him rise speedily through confession and cleanse himself by penance, for the mercy of God is rich in pardoning the sinner who wills to turn to the way of justice, as the prophet says: {{< verse ref="Ezek 33:11" >}}I desire not the death of the wicked, but that the wicked turn from his way, and live.{{< /verse >}} And again in another place, concerning the will of God and the final act: {{< verse ref="Ezek 18:21" >}}But if the wicked do penance for all his sins which he hath committed, and keep all my commandments, and do judgment, and justice, living he shall live, and shall not die.{{< /verse >}} The envy of the devil persuades us to sin; but the mercy of the Lord, who desires our salvation, urges us to turn from sin to the way of justice, that we may be made worthy of eternal glory with His saints. And this we ought most certainly to know and to believe: that all our works and thoughts shall be judged on the last day. For sinners who will not abandon their sins and obey the commandments of God, there shall be eternal torments in everlasting fire with the devil and his angels. But those who turn with their whole heart to Almighty God, living in charity and obedient to the commandments of God, shall have eternal glory, blessedness, and joy, with Christ and His saints, world without end.
{{< /section >}}
