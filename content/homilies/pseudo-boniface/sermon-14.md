## Original Text

{{< section "1" >}}
Gratias Deo Salvatori nostro, qui nos sanos pervenire permisit ad celebrandum diem Dominicum paschalis solemnitatis. Hodie in universo mundo in sanctis Christi Ecclesiis resurrectio nostri Salvatoris Iesu Christi Domini nostri celebratur. Ideo decet, fratres charissimi, ut aliquid de tam magna solemnitate loquamur. Apparuit autem Iesus Christus in carne, mori dignatus est ex voluntate, resurrexit ex potestate, et ostendit exemplo quod nobis promisit in praemio. Solus ille in tempore mortuus est, et tamen solus minime surrexit. Nam scriptum est: Multa corpora sanctorum quae dormierunt resurrexerunt. Ecce cum Deo homines resurrexisse cognoscimus, et quos puros fuisse homines non dubitamus. Si igitur membra nostri Redemptoris sumus, praesumamus in nobis quod gestum constat in capite, quod est Christus Iesus. Ille enim, sicut Paulus apostolus dicit: Mortuus est propter delicta nostra, et resurrexit propter iustificationem nostram. O magna clementia Redemptoris nostri unigeniti Filii Dei, et Salvatoris nostri Iesu Christi Domini nostri, qui venit in hunc mundum peccatores salvos facere, quia omnes homines ante adventum Salvatoris per culpam rei erant primae praevaricationis Adami et Evae uxoris eius!
{{< /section >}}

{{< section "2" >}}
Audistis saepe, fratres charissimi, quomodo primi parentes nostri Dei praeceptum transgressi sunt, et nunc breviter dicamus. Erant enim constituti in paradiso felicitatis tantae, ut si voluissent in illa paradisi beatitudine custodire ut a fructu prohibitae arboris se abstinerent, semper absque morte et miseria fuissent. Praecepit enim eis Dominus Deus, ut de fructu unius arboris non manducarent, ne totum eis licitum esse putarent, et similes se Deo crederent, si nulla lege tenerentur, et paradisus ille in terra positus est, et tamen tantae, sicut supra diximus, felicitatis, ut si Dei praeceptum custodirent, semper absque morte et miseria fuissent, donec translati essent in regna caelestia. Sed antiquus hostis invidendo de tanta gloria hominis, quia noluit ut homo tantam haberet felicitatem, de qua ipse per suam superbiam deiectus est, quia a Deo bonus fuit creatus, sed per suam superbiam cecidit de caelo, et ideo invidebat de hominis gloria, quia ille perdiderat suam, propterea tentando per serpentem suasit mulieri ut ederet de fructu illius ligni de quo Dominus praecepit ut non ederent, et dixit: Si gustaveritis de ligno, aperientur oculi vestri, et eritis sicut dii, scientes bonum et malum. Consensit mulier suasioni, et comedit, et dedit viro, et comedit ipse, et tunc post peccatum ambo deiecti sunt de paradiso in hanc miseram vitam, et omnis homo propter illam transgressionem sub mortis imperio tenebatur, et omnes ad inferni claustra descenderunt, iusti tamen ibi non arserunt, sed tamen fuerunt in inferno, sedentes in tenebris et umbra mortis, et nullus hominum potuit illam damnationem a se excutere, sed omnes in inferno detenti sunt, donec Iesus Christus Dominus noster per uterum Mariae semper virginis nasci in mundo dignatus est, ut idem fieret hominum liberator qui fuit creator, et in eadem carne, quam pro nostra omniumque salute suscepit, multas iniurias a perfidis Iudaeorum populis sustinuit, non ut se vindicare non potuisset, sed exemplum nobis patientiae exhibuit, ut cum tolerantia tribulationes et iniurias patiamur. Ille tentationes diaboli sustinuit, et eum sua virtute superavit, et in abyssum eum sua divinitate mergere potuit, et tamen nisi mansuetudinis verba non respondit. Toleravit a perfidis Iudaeis multa blasphemia verborum et insuper flagella, sputa, alapas, spineam coronam, et incisiones multas, et post ista omnia et alia multa, in cruce positus, clavis corpus suum perforari non renuit, animam ponendo mortem sustinuit, nulla necessitate, sed propria voluntate in sepulcro carnem reposuit, et ad inferna descendit, et mortis principem colligavit, id est, diabolum, vectes ferreos portarum inferni confregit, et omnes iustos inde eripiens, in libertatem pristinam revocavit, resurgensque a morte, lucem salutis aeternae nobis in se credentibus demonstravit.
{{< /section >}}

{{< section "3" >}}
Hanc resurrectionis eius gloriam annua devotione venerantes hodie colimus. Laetemur ergo, dilectissimi, et exsultemus in Domino, quia in ista sancta solemnitate Redemptor noster resurgendo vitam nobis immortalem ostendit, et per suam gratiam daturus est, si nolumus transmigrare ad virtutes a vitiis. Idcirco mundemus nos ab omni iniquitate et serviamus Domino Deo nostro Iesu Christo, quia ille pro nobis suum sanctum sanguinem fudit, ut nos redimeret ab omni iniquitate. Ideo debemus sancte et iuste et pie vivere in hoc saeculo, et nihil ante Dei amorem ponere, qui nos in tantum dilexit, ut pro nobis, sicut diximus, ipse moreretur. Nunc, fratres charissimi, eius resurrectionem gaudentes celebremus, et ei puro corde et casto corpore, nosmetipsos praeparemus, ut per haec festa paschalia, quae hic agimus, pervenire ad caelestia mereamur, et ibi semper beate vivere cum sanctis angelis et cum omnibus iustis, sicut ipse Dominus dixit: Tunc iusti fulgebunt sicut sol in regno Patris eorum. Ad illam aeternam laetitiam nos perducere dignetur, qui nos creavit Iesus Christus Dominus noster, qui regnat cum Patre in unitate Spiritus sancti Deus per omnia saecula saeculorum. Amen.
{{< /section >}}

## English Translation

{{< section "1" >}}
Thanks be to God our Savior, who has allowed us to arrive in health to celebrate the Lord's Day of the paschal feast. Today throughout the whole world in the holy churches of Christ the resurrection of our Savior Jesus Christ our Lord is celebrated. It is fitting, then, dearest brethren, that we say something about so great a solemnity. Jesus Christ appeared in the flesh, deigned to die by his own will, rose again by his own power, and showed in his example what he promised us as our reward. He alone died in time, and yet it was not he alone who rose. For it is written: {{< verse ref="Matt 27:52" >}}Many bodies of the saints that had slept arose.{{< /verse >}} We know, then, that human beings rose together with God — human beings whom we do not doubt to have been purely human. If we are therefore members of our Redeemer, let us presume in ourselves what is established as having happened in our head, who is Christ Jesus. For as the Apostle Paul says: {{< verse ref="Rom 4:25" >}}He was delivered up for our sins, and rose again for our justification.{{< /verse >}} O great is the mercy of our Redeemer, the only-begotten Son of God, our Savior Jesus Christ our Lord, who came into this world to save sinners — for all people before the coming of the Savior were guilty through the fault of the first transgression of Adam and Eve his wife!
{{< /section >}}

{{< section "2" >}}
You have often heard, dearest brethren, how our first parents transgressed the commandment of God; let us now say it briefly. They had been placed in a paradise of such great happiness that if they had chosen to keep themselves in that blessedness of paradise by abstaining from the fruit of the forbidden tree, they would have lived forever without death or misery. For the Lord God commanded them not to eat the fruit of one tree, lest they think everything was permitted to them and believe themselves equal to God if they were bound by no law. That paradise was set on earth, yet of such happiness, as we said above, that if they had kept God's commandment they would always have lived without death or misery, until they were translated to the heavenly kingdoms. But the ancient enemy, envying the great glory of man — because he did not want man to enjoy the happiness from which he himself had been cast down by his own pride, for he was created good by God but fell from heaven through his own pride, and therefore envied man's glory because he had lost his own — tempted him, persuading the woman through the serpent to eat of the fruit of the tree that the Lord had commanded them not to eat. And he said: {{< verse ref="Gen 3:5" >}}If you eat of the tree, your eyes shall be opened: and you shall be as Gods, knowing good and evil.{{< /verse >}} The woman consented to his persuasion and ate, and gave to her husband, and he ate; and then after the sin both were cast out of paradise into this wretched life, and every person on account of that transgression was held under the dominion of death. All descended to the gates of hell; the just did not burn there, yet they were in hell, sitting in darkness and the shadow of death, and no human being could shake off that condemnation. All were held in hell until our Lord Jesus Christ deigned to be born into the world through the womb of Mary ever-virgin — so that the same one who was their Creator might become their Liberator. In that same flesh which he took on for our salvation and the salvation of all, he endured many injuries from the treacherous Jewish people — not because he could not have avenged himself, but to give us an example of patience, so that we might endure tribulations and injuries with forbearance. He endured the temptations of the devil and overcame him by his own power; he could have plunged him into the abyss by his divinity, and yet answered him with nothing but words of meekness. He bore many blasphemies of words from the treacherous Jews, and moreover scourges, spittle, blows, a crown of thorns, and many wounds; and after all these things and many more besides, placed on the cross, he did not refuse to have his body pierced by nails; laying down his soul he endured death; by his own will and not by necessity he laid his flesh in the tomb; he descended to the underworld, bound the prince of death, that is the devil, broke the iron bars of the gates of hell, and snatching all the just from there, called them back to their former freedom. Rising from death, he showed to those who believe in him the light of eternal salvation.
{{< /section >}}

{{< section "3" >}}
Honoring the glory of his resurrection with yearly devotion, we celebrate it today. Let us therefore rejoice, beloved, and exult in the Lord, because in this holy feast our Redeemer, by rising, showed us immortal life and will bestow it through his grace, if we are willing to cross over from vices to virtues. Let us therefore cleanse ourselves from all iniquity and serve the Lord our God Jesus Christ, for he shed his holy blood for us to redeem us from all iniquity. We must therefore live in this world in a holy, just, and devout manner, and place nothing before the love of God, who loved us so greatly as to die for us himself, as we have said. Now, dearest brethren, let us celebrate his resurrection with joy, and prepare ourselves for him with a pure heart and a chaste body, so that through these paschal celebrations which we keep here, we may merit to reach the things of heaven and live there always in blessedness with the holy angels and with all the just, as the Lord himself said: {{< verse ref="Matt 13:43" >}}Then shall the just shine as the sun in the kingdom of their Father.{{< /verse >}} May he who created us, Jesus Christ our Lord, deign to lead us to that eternal joy — who reigns with the Father in the unity of the Holy Spirit, God for ever and ever. Amen.
{{< /section >}}
